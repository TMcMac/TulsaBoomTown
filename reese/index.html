Tulsa has a tech ecosystem that is rapidly changing as it marches forward. As we experience this growth, we are seeing a gap between wonderful tech companies, and well-qualified entry-level canidates emerging on Tulsa's tech scene. As recent graduates of Holberton School's Foundations Program, we are trying to bridge that ever-growing gap with this website.

Oppertunities to Learn and Grow

Tulsa Remote:

You're looking for something new, Tulsa Remote is looking for great people to join the Tulsa community. Currently offering $10,000 cash, free desk space, exclusive perks / events and of course, a welcoming community.

Holberton School:

Located in the Downtown Tulsa Arts District, Holberton School is Oklahoma's premiere coding school. Campus facilities are open to students 24hrs a day, 7 days a week. They offer a spacious computer lab with over 30 work stations and various conference rooms, quiet spaces and nooks. Learn by doing, learn from each other!

36D North:

36 Degrees North is the central gathering point for Tulsa's startup founders, small business owners, remote workers and innovative community leaders. We provide high-quality workspace in the Tulsa Arts District, foster a tight-knit community, and curate connections to helpful resources, so entrepreneurs can grow and create long-lasting, impactful businesses.

5th Floor:

New incubator, coming soon, brought to you by 36 and city of Tulsa. A plan has been proposed to use $1.25 million in CARES Act funding to create a flexible work space on the fifth floor of City Hall. 36 Degrees North, which has sites in the Tulsa Arts District, would pay about $480,000 a year in rent.
